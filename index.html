Part 1

The Struggler's Blueprint

Objective: To move from the trauma of being "caught" (Discovery) to the freedom of "choosing to tell" (Disclosure) by implementing physical, emotional, and spiritual boundaries.

When we are struggling with a habit like phone or pornography addiction, our brain’s "biological brakes" are often overwhelmed by the "dopamine fire hose." Before we can do deep soul work, we need to create a safe environment and allow the neurological system to reset.

  • Bedroom Boundaries: Remove all internet-connected devices from the bedroom. It is reserved exclusively for rest and real-world intimacy.
  • Filter Architecture: Install accountability software (e.g., Covenant Eyes, Canopy). These are "speed bumps" to give your brain time to choose truth over an impulse.
  • Analog Resets: Designate a specific 24-hour period each week to remain entirely offline to reset dopamine sensitivity.

"Turn my eyes from looking at worthless things; and give me life in your ways." - Psalm 119:37

We believe that shame is a curtain we hang over our hearts to hide our true selves. Healing is impossible in the dark.

  • Identify a Safe Circle: Find a mentor or care team that operates under three rules: No Judgment, No Fixing, and Absolute Confidentiality.
  • Full Disclosure vs. Trickle-Truth: Voluntarily pull down the "curtain" by naming the struggle clearly. Tell the truth to get healthy, not just to get out of trouble.
  • Renounce the Sales Pitch: Identify the lie you are believing (e.g., "I can have intimacy without vulnerability") and replace it with the truth of your identity.

Pornography is often a "release valve" for internal pressure. You must address the "soil" where the addiction grew.

  • Pressure Cooker Check: Identify the "HALT" triggers—Hungry, Angry, Lonely, Tired—that drive you to numb out.
  • The Debt List (Forgiveness): List those you resent. Unforgiveness creates "digital static". Releasing the debt lowers the internal pressure.

A vine that grows on the ground will rot; it needs a structure to lean on. Build your "3-Circle Plan":

1. Inner Circle

Define specific behaviors you are renouncing (e.g., viewing pornography). This is crossing the line.

2. Middle Circle

Identify warning signs like being on your phone after 11 PM, or isolating when stressed.

3. Outer Circle

Focus on "analog" joys: physical exercise, outdoor hobbies, or serving at church.

The final goal of restoration is to have Surplus Value—the extra emotional and spiritual energy you gain when you are no longer wasting your life hiding secrets.

  • Protector of the Sandbox: Shift from being a consumer of people (viewing them as products) to a protector of your family and the next generation.
  • Repairer of the Ruins: Your story of freedom becomes a blueprint that helps others find their way out from behind their own curtains.
Part 2

The Spouse's Blueprint

Objective: To provide a sanctuary and stabilizing framework for the spouse who has experienced the trauma of discovery or disclosure, moving from the initial "explosion" toward personal safety and restored agency.

In the wake of betrayal, your brain is likely in a "fight, flight, or freeze" state. Our first priority is to move you from a state of emergency to a state of safety.

  • Prioritize Self-Care over Investigation: Stop the "detective work." Constantly searching behind the "curtains" keeps your brain in trauma. Focus on sleep, hydration, and nutrition.
  • Define Physical Boundaries: Establish exactly what you need to feel safe in your home right now.
  • The Safe Circle: Seek circles of women who operate under the rules of No Judgment, No Fixing, and Absolute Safety.

Betrayal creates a "shattering" of your story. The restorative process helps you pick up the pieces and see them clearly.

  • Dismantle the Shame: Recognize that your partner’s struggle is a biological and spiritual "choke-hold" on their life. It is not a reflection of your worth or beauty.
  • Disclosure over Discovery: Require a transition where your partner voluntarily brings the truth into the light.

The intense internal pressure and pain of betrayal must be released healthily so it doesn't manifest as destructive bitterness.

  • Practice Lament: Give yourself permission to be angry and grieved.
  • The Debt Assessment: Forgiveness is not "fixing" them. It is a spiritual transaction to release the debt to God so it stops rotting your own soul.

You need a "trellis" for your own life that does not depend on your husband’s choices or progress.

  • Build Your 3-Circle Plan: Define your Non-Negotiables (Inner), warning signs (Middle), and Analog Joys (Outer).
  • Surplus Value & Protection: As you heal, you gain surplus emotional strength to lead your children with wisdom, becoming a Protector of the Sandbox.

Part 4

Leadership Roles – The Support Team

In a "church without curtains," recovery thrives in community. However, different roles provide different types of architectural support. It is crucial that these roles are filled by different people.

Mentor

The "Spiritual Architect"

  • Primary Focus: The Roots & Soil (Why are you clicking?)
  • Goal: Internal Transformation, Heart Healing, Identity
  • Tools Used: Freedom Steps, Restorative Prayer, 3-Circle Plan
  • Frequency: Deep, structured sessions (Weekly/Monthly)
  • Qualification: Must have "Surplus Value" and sustained freedom.

Accountability Partner

The "Speed Bump"

  • Primary Focus: The Brakes & Behavior (How do we stop the click?)
  • Goal: External Stabilization, Tactical Daily Safety
  • Tools Used: Software Monitoring, Filter Architecture, Daily Logs
  • Frequency: Frequent, brief check-ins (Daily/As-needed)
  • Qualification: Can be a fellow struggler fighting in the trenches.

Why Must They Be Separate?

  • 1. Specialization: One manages the urgent tactical needs (Did you look at porn today?), while the other manages the important heart transformation (Who do you need to forgive?).
  • 2. Preventing "Reporting Shame": If a Mentor also receives daily software reports, a "slip" can make the participant feel they failed their spiritual guide, leading to "trickle-truth" and new curtains. Separating them creates a safe space.
  • 3. Expanding the Circle: Ecclesiastes 4:12 reminds us that "a threefold cord is not quickly broken." Utilizing both roles expands your load-bearing relationships.
Part 5

The Care Team Blueprint (SOP for Disclosures)

Objective: To equip mentors, care team members, and church leaders to handle disclosures of sexual sin with grace, ensuring the participant moves from isolation to safety without experiencing "reporting shame" or judgment.

When a participant bravely tells a secret for the first time, your initial reaction will either build trust or cause them to hang the curtain back up.

  • Don't Panic: Stay calm, steady, and empathetic. Do not show shock or disappointment.
  • Thank Them: The very first thing you should say is: "Thank you for being brave enough to tell the truth. That took incredible strength."
  • Validate the Step: Remind them that pulling down the curtain and choosing disclosure is the only way to break the neurological choke-hold.

The goal of a disclosure conversation is heart healing, not gathering evidence.

  • Focus on the "Why": Do not ask for graphic details. Ask: "What hunger, pain, or pressure were you trying to hide in that moment?"
  • Reinforce the Three Rules: Remind them they are in a safe circle governed by: No Judgment, No Fixing, and Absolute Safety (Confidentiality).
  • The Leader's Heart: You cannot lead others where you are not willing to go. Be a "truth-teller" yourself to give the group permission to drop their curtains.
  • Guide the Trellis: Gently help them update their 3-Circle Plan, identify new "slippery slopes", and lean into their "analog joys".

While Grace Community Church is committed to being a safe place, the safety of children supersedes all confidentiality. If a disclosure involves images of children (CSAM), the standard process must immediately stop.

  • Halt the Conversation: State clearly: "I need to stop you right there. For the safety of children, our policy requires us to report this. I cannot keep this confidential."
  • Mandatory Reporting: Contact local law enforcement immediately. Do not investigate this yourself.
  • Leadership Escalation: Notify the Senior Pastor and church legal counsel/elder board.
  • Boundary Enforcement: The individual must be immediately removed from any volunteer roles involving minors.

If an individual discloses a struggle with adult pornography (no minors) but volunteers in ministry, handle it with grace and recovery rather than punishment:

  • Validate Their Honesty: A leader who is honest is vastly safer than a leader who is hiding.
  • The Healing Sabbatical: Ask the individual to take a temporary, grace-filled break from serving. This is an architectural measure, not punitive.
  • Lowering the Pressure: Explain: "To lead children well, you need 'Surplus Value.' We are taking this responsibility off your plate so you can focus entirely on getting healthy."
  • The Pathway Back: Establish clear milestones for returning based on sustained periods of living in their "Outer Circle."
